The Climb

Col de Vence rises from the medieval hilltop town of Vence at 321m to its summit at 962m — a true Category 1 ascent across 9.7 kilometres.

Paris-Nice, the Race to the Sun, has featured the Col de Vence as both a summit finish and an intermediate climb on the road to the Côte d'Azur.

Pro teams including INEOS Grenadiers and AG2R Citroën use the Col de Vence for training camps, drawn by its proximity to Nice and consistent road conditions.

The Strava KOM stands at 23 minutes and 58 seconds — an average speed of 24.3 km/h sustained across a 6.5% mean gradient.

From the summit on a clear day, the panorama stretches across the Var valley to the full arc of the Mediterranean coastline.

The D2 road surface is well-maintained, wide, and generally low-traffic — making the Col de Vence accessible year-round except during rare winter closures.
